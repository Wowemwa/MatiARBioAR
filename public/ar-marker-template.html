üìñ About AR Markers

AR markers are special images that your camera recognizes to display 3D models. Each species in our biodiversity database has a unique marker that, when scanned, reveals an augmented reality representation of that species.

How to Use These Markers

  1. Print this page on white paper (color recommended)
  2. Cut out each marker along the borders
  3. Laminate for durability (optional but recommended)
  4. Open the species AR viewer on your phone
  5. Point your camera at the marker
  6. Watch the 3D model appear!

‚ú® Best Practices

üñ®Ô∏è Printing

Use high-quality color printing on white paper. Minimum A4 size recommended.

üìè Size

Print markers at least 15cm x 15cm. Larger markers are easier to detect.

üé® Contrast

Ensure high contrast between marker and background. Use dark markers on light surfaces.

üí° Lighting

Good lighting is essential. Avoid glare and shadows on the marker.

üì± Distance

Hold phone 20-50cm from marker. Not too close, not too far.

üîÑ Angle

Keep marker flat and parallel to camera. Avoid extreme angles.

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Notes

Marker Quality Matters

Damaged, wrinkled, or low-quality printed markers may not work properly. For best results, laminate printed markers to protect them from wear and tear.

Unique Patterns Required

Each species must have a unique marker pattern. Using duplicate markers will cause confusion in AR detection.

Camera Permissions

The AR viewer requires camera access. Make sure to allow camera permissions when prompted by your browser.

üîß Troubleshooting

If marker isn't detecting:

  1. Improve lighting - use natural or bright indoor light
  2. Flatten the marker - remove wrinkles or curves
  3. Clean your camera lens
  4. Move to appropriate distance (20-50cm)
  5. Ensure marker is not partially covered
  6. Try printing a new copy if marker is damaged

If 3D model doesn't appear:

  1. Ensure marker is detected (check for tracking indicators)
  2. Wait a few seconds for model to load
  3. Check internet connection (needed to load 3D model)
  4. Try refreshing the AR viewer page
  5. Verify the species has AR content uploaded

üé® Generating Custom Markers

Using AR.js Pattern Generator:

  1. Visit: AR.js Pattern Generator
  2. Upload your species image or create a custom design
  3. Adjust contrast and borders as needed
  4. Download the .patt file (pattern file)
  5. Download the marker image (PNG)
  6. Upload both files in the admin panel
  7. Print this template with your new marker

Design Tips:

  • Use high contrast colors (black/white works best)
  • Add a border around the marker
  • Keep designs simple and recognizable
  • Test different lighting conditions
  • Make each species marker visually distinct
